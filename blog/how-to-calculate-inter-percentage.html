How to Calculate Intermediate Percentage — Part 1 & Part 2 Combined

Calculating your Intermediate (HSSC) percentage is essential for university admissions in Pakistan. Unlike Matric, your Inter result combines two separate exams — Part 1 (11th class) and Part 2 (12th class). This guide explains exactly how to calculate your combined percentage.

Understanding the Inter Marking System

The Intermediate exam in Pakistan is divided into two parts:

  • Part 1 (HSSC-I): Taken after 11th class, typically covers 550 marks for science groups
  • Part 2 (HSSC-II): Taken after 12th class, also typically 550 marks for science groups
  • Combined Total: 1100 marks (for most science group students)

The total marks can vary depending on your board and group (Pre-Engineering, Pre-Medical, ICS, Commerce, Arts).

Step-by-Step Calculation

Step 1: Note Your Part 1 Marks

Find your HSSC Part 1 result card. Write down your total obtained marks. For example:

  • English: 65/100
  • Urdu: 70/100
  • Physics: 72/100
  • Chemistry: 68/100
  • Mathematics: 80/100
  • Islamiat: 40/50

Part 1 Obtained: 395 / 550

Step 2: Note Your Part 2 Marks

Similarly, write down your Part 2 obtained marks:

  • English: 70/100
  • Physics: 75/100
  • Chemistry: 72/100
  • Mathematics: 88/100
  • Pak Studies: 38/50
  • Computer/Elective: 42/50

Part 2 Obtained: 385 / 550

Step 3: Add Both Parts Together

Combine your marks from Part 1 and Part 2:

Total Obtained = 395 + 385 = 780

Total Marks = 550 + 550 = 1100

Step 4: Calculate Percentage

Apply the percentage formula:

Percentage = (780 / 1100) × 100 = 70.91%

Inter Grading System

Pakistani boards use the following grades for Intermediate:

  • A1 (80% and above): Exceptional
  • A (70–79%): Excellent
  • B (60–69%): Very Good
  • C (50–59%): Good
  • D (40–49%): Satisfactory
  • E (33–39%): Pass

Why Your Inter Percentage Matters

Your Intermediate percentage is crucial for several reasons:

  • University Admissions: Most Pakistani universities use your Inter marks as a major component of their merit formula (typically 40–60% weightage)
  • Scholarship Eligibility: Many scholarships require minimum Inter percentages (usually 60%+)
  • Merit Lists: Board-level and university merit lists rank students by their Inter percentage
  • NUST Aggregate: NUST gives 40% weightage to your Inter marks in their aggregate formula

What If I Only Have Part 1 Results?

If you've only completed Part 1 so far, you can still calculate your Part 1 percentage separately:

Part 1 Percentage = (Part 1 Obtained / Part 1 Total) × 100

Some universities accept provisional admission based on Part 1 results. However, your final admission will depend on the combined Part 1 + Part 2 percentage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are practical marks included in the Inter percentage?

Yes, practical marks are part of your total marks. For science subjects like Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Computer Science, practical exams carry additional marks that are included in your total.

Do Part 1 and Part 2 have equal weightage?

Yes, both parts carry equal marks (typically 550 each). Your overall percentage is based on the combined total of both parts. Some universities may weigh Part 2 slightly more, but the standard percentage calculation treats both equally.

Can I improve my Inter percentage?

Yes, most boards allow improvement exams. You can re-appear in specific subjects to improve your marks. The higher marks are then used to calculate your updated percentage. Check with your board for improvement exam schedules and rules.

How is the Inter percentage used for NUST admission?

NUST gives 40% weightage to your Inter marks in their aggregate formula: Aggregate = (Matric % × 0.10) + (Inter % × 0.40) + (NET % × 0.50). Use our NUST Calculator to see your estimated aggregate.
