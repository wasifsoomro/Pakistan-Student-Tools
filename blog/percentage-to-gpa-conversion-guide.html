Percentage to GPA Conversion — Complete Guide for Pakistani Students

Whether you're applying to a Pakistani university, a foreign institution, or a job that requires GPA, you may need to convert your board percentage to a GPA. This guide covers all the formulas and tables you need.

Standard Conversion Formula (4.0 Scale)

The simplest and most widely accepted formula:

GPA = Percentage / 25

Examples:

Percentage GPA (4.0 Scale) Letter Grade Classification
90–100% 3.6–4.0 A / A+ Distinction
80–89% 3.2–3.56 A- / B+ First Division
70–79% 2.8–3.16 B / B- First Division
60–69% 2.4–2.76 C+ / C First Division
50–59% 2.0–2.36 C- / D+ Second Division
40–49% 1.6–1.96 D Third Division
33–39% 1.32–1.56 E Pass

Conversion for 5.0 Scale

Some Pakistani universities (like University of Karachi) use a 5.0 GPA scale:

GPA = Percentage / 20

Example: 80% = 80 / 20 = 4.0 GPA on a 5.0 scale

HEC Grading Guidelines

The Higher Education Commission provides a standard grading framework that most Pakistani universities follow:

  • A+ (4.0): 85% and above — Exceptional
  • A (4.0): 80–84% — Excellent
  • A- (3.67): 75–79%
  • B+ (3.33): 71–74%
  • B (3.0): 68–70%
  • B- (2.67): 64–67%
  • C+ (2.33): 61–63%
  • C (2.0): 58–60%
  • C- (1.67): 55–57%
  • D (1.0): 50–54%
  • F (0.0): Below 50%

Note: Individual universities may have slightly different grade boundaries.

When Do You Need This Conversion?

  • Foreign university applications: US, UK, and Australian universities often want GPA on a 4.0 scale
  • Scholarship applications: Many international scholarships require GPA-based eligibility
  • Job applications: Some multinational companies use GPA cutoffs
  • Graduate school: MS/PhD programs may require minimum GPA thresholds

Board Percentage to Division

Pakistani boards classify results into divisions:

  • Distinction: 80% and above
  • First Division: 60–79%
  • Second Division: 45–59%
  • Third Division: 33–44%

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 3.0 GPA good for Pakistani students?

A 3.0 GPA (75%) is considered good and falls in First Division. For competitive programs and scholarships, aim for 3.5+ (87.5%). For most job requirements, 2.5+ (62.5%) is the minimum cutoff.

Do US universities accept Pakistani percentage?

Most US universities use credential evaluation services (like WES or ECE) that convert Pakistani percentages to the US GPA scale. The conversion may differ slightly from the simple ÷25 formula, so check with the specific evaluation service.

What's the difference between GPA and CGPA?

GPA (Grade Point Average) usually refers to a single semester's average, while CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the overall average across all semesters. For conversion purposes, most people need their CGPA converted to percentage or vice versa.

Can I convert my Matric percentage to GPA?

Yes, use the formula GPA = Percentage / 25 (for 4.0 scale). For example, if your Matric percentage is 82%, your equivalent GPA is 82 / 25 = 3.28.
