Can I Improve My Matric/Inter Result? — Rules & Process Explained

Didn't score as well as you hoped in Matric or Intermediate? You're not alone, and the good news is that most Pakistani boards allow improvement exams. This guide explains the rules, process, and everything you need to know about improving your board results.

Yes, You Can Improve — Here's How

Most Pakistani educational boards allow students who have already passed to appear in improvement exams. The key rules are:

  • You must have already passed the original exam (improvement is not the same as supplementary)
  • You can improve specific subjects — you don't need to retake all subjects
  • The higher marks count — if your improvement marks are lower, your original marks are kept
  • Usually limited to 3 subjects per attempt (varies by board)

Improvement vs Supplementary — What's the Difference?

Feature Improvement Exam Supplementary Exam
Who can take it Students who already passed Students who failed 1-3 subjects
Purpose To improve marks/grade To pass failed subjects
Risk No risk — higher marks are kept Must pass or remain failed
Timing Next annual exam cycle Usually 2-3 months after results
Subjects allowed Up to 3 (varies by board) Only failed subjects (max 3)

Board-Wise Improvement Rules

Punjab Boards (Lahore, Rawalpindi, Gujranwala, etc.)

  • Improvement allowed in up to 3 subjects
  • Must apply during the next annual examination
  • Both Matric and Inter improvement available
  • Result card shows improved marks with "Improvement" stamp

More details: Punjab Board page

Sindh Boards (Karachi, Hyderabad, Sukkur, etc.)

  • Improvement exams available for passed students
  • Can improve in selected subjects
  • Apply through the respective board office

More details: Sindh Board page

FBISE (Federal Board)

  • Improvement allowed in up to 3 subjects
  • Must appear in the next annual exam
  • Original marks are retained if improvement marks are lower

More details: FBISE Board page

KPK, Balochistan, AJK Boards

  • Most allow improvement exams with similar rules
  • Check with your specific board for exact policies

Step-by-Step Process to Apply

  1. Check the schedule: Visit your board's website for improvement exam dates and registration deadlines
  2. Collect documents: Original mark sheet, CNIC/B-Form copy, passport photos
  3. Fill the application form: Available at board office or downloadable from website
  4. Select subjects: Choose which subjects you want to improve (max 3)
  5. Pay the fee: Improvement exam fee is typically PKR 1,000-3,000 per subject
  6. Submit before deadline: Late applications may be accepted with additional fee
  7. Appear in exam: Prepare well and appear at your designated center
  8. Check results: Results typically come 2-3 months after the exam

Is Improvement Worth It?

Consider improvement if:

  • You're close to a grade boundary: Going from B to A or A to A1 can help with university admissions
  • Your marks hurt your aggregate: Since Matric carries 10% and Inter carries 40% in most formulas, improving these directly boosts your university merit
  • You have time: Improvement exams happen in the next annual cycle, so plan accordingly
  • Specific weak subjects: If you know exactly which subjects dragged down your percentage

Impact on University Admissions

Universities accept improvement marks for merit calculations. However:

  • The improved result card may show an "Improvement" marking
  • Some competitive programs may note the improvement, but your marks are treated equally
  • Timing matters — make sure your improved results arrive before admission deadlines

Frequently Asked Questions

Will my original marks decrease if improvement marks are lower?

No. This is the best part about improvement exams — your original marks are always preserved. If your improvement marks are lower, your original (higher) marks remain on your record.

Can I improve both Matric and Inter at the same time?

Yes, you can apply for improvement in both Matric and Inter subjects simultaneously, as long as you've passed both. However, managing preparation for multiple subjects across two levels is challenging.

How many times can I attempt improvement?

Most boards allow improvement once (in the next annual exam cycle). Some boards may allow additional attempts. Check with your specific board office for their policy.

Do universities know I took an improvement exam?

Your result card will typically show that it's an improvement result. However, universities use the marks for aggregate calculation regardless. The percentage is what matters for merit, not whether it came from a regular or improvement exam.
