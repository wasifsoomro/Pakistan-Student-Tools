Top 10 Engineering Universities in Pakistan 2026 — Admission & Merit Guide

Choosing the right engineering university is one of the biggest decisions Pakistani students make after their Intermediate exams. This guide ranks the top 10 engineering universities in Pakistan for 2026, covering their admission requirements, entry tests, and what makes each one stand out.

1. NUST — National University of Sciences and Technology

Location: Islamabad (main campus), Rawalpindi, Karachi, Quetta

Entry Test: NET (NUST Entry Test)

Aggregate Formula: Matric 10% + Inter 40% + NET 50%

Top Programs: Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, Mechanical Engineering

Why Choose NUST: Consistently ranked #1 in Pakistan by QS Rankings. Excellent faculty, modern labs, and strong industry connections. The SEECS school is particularly renowned for CS and EE.

Calculate your chances with our NUST Aggregate Calculator.

2. GIKI — Ghulam Ishaq Khan Institute

Location: Topi, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa

Entry Test: Own admission test

Top Programs: Engineering Sciences, Computer Science, Electrical Engineering

Why Choose GIKI: Residential campus with a focused academic environment. Known for producing top-quality engineers. Smaller class sizes mean more personalized attention.

3. LUMS — Lahore University of Management Sciences

Location: Lahore

Entry Test: SAT or LUMS-specific test

Top Programs: Computer Science, Electrical Engineering

Why Choose LUMS: Liberal arts approach to engineering education. Excellent CS program, strong entrepreneurship culture, and generous financial aid program (National Outreach Programme).

4. PIEAS — Pakistan Institute of Engineering and Applied Sciences

Location: Islamabad

Entry Test: Own written test + interview

Top Programs: Nuclear Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Computer & Information Sciences

Why Choose PIEAS: Extremely selective (one of the hardest to get into), fully funded education, and close ties to Pakistan's strategic programs. Graduates are highly sought after.

5. UET Lahore — University of Engineering and Technology

Location: Lahore (main campus), plus sub-campuses

Entry Test: ECAT (Engineering College Admission Test)

Aggregate Formula: Matric 10% + Inter 40% + ECAT 50% (approximate)

Top Programs: Civil Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering

Why Choose UET: Pakistan's oldest and most established engineering university. Massive alumni network, affordable fee structure, and PEC-accredited programs.

6. FAST-NUCES — National University of Computer and Emerging Sciences

Location: Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi, Peshawar, Faisalabad, Chiniot-Faisalabad

Entry Test: FAST Entry Test (NAT-based or own test)

Top Programs: Computer Science, Software Engineering, Electrical Engineering

Why Choose FAST: Best known for Computer Science in Pakistan. Multiple campuses across the country, rigorous academic program, and strong placement record in the tech industry.

7. COMSATS University

Location: Islamabad (main), Lahore, Abbottabad, Wah, and others

Entry Test: NTS NAT or own test

Top Programs: Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, Bioinformatics

Why Choose COMSATS: Good balance of quality and affordability. Islamabad campus is particularly well-regarded. Offers scholarships and has a growing research output.

8. NED University — NED University of Engineering and Technology

Location: Karachi

Entry Test: NED own entry test

Top Programs: Civil Engineering, Architecture, Petroleum Engineering

Why Choose NED: The leading engineering university in Sindh. Very affordable for Sindh domicile students, with a strong reputation in Civil and Petroleum Engineering.

9. Air University

Location: Islamabad, Kamra

Entry Test: Own entry test or NAT

Top Programs: Aerospace Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Computer Science

Why Choose Air University: One of the few universities offering Aerospace Engineering in Pakistan. Affiliated with Pakistan Air Force, offering unique industry exposure.

10. Bahria University

Location: Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi

Entry Test: Own entry test

Top Programs: Computer Engineering, Software Engineering, Electrical Engineering

Why Choose Bahria: Good infrastructure, modern campus in Islamabad, and connections to Pakistan Navy. Offers a range of engineering and computing programs.

Entry Test Comparison

Different universities accept different entry tests. Here's a quick comparison:

  • ECAT: Required for UET Lahore and other Punjab-based public engineering universities
  • NET: Only for NUST
  • NAT: Accepted by FAST, COMSATS, and several private universities
  • SAT: Accepted by LUMS and some international programs

Frequently Asked Questions

Which engineering university is easiest to get into?

Among the top 10, COMSATS and Bahria University generally have more accessible merit requirements compared to NUST, GIKI, or PIEAS. However, "easy" is relative — you still need strong Inter marks and a decent entry test score.

Do I need ECAT for NUST?

No, NUST has its own test called NET (NUST Entry Test). ECAT is for UET Lahore and other Punjab-based universities. These are separate tests with different structures.

Which university is best for Computer Science?

FAST-NUCES, NUST (SEECS), and LUMS are widely considered the top 3 for Computer Science in Pakistan. FAST is especially known for its rigorous CS curriculum.
