NUST Aggregate Formula Explained

NUST uses the following weightage to calculate aggregate scores for admissions:

  • Matric (SSC): 10% of your Matric percentage
  • Intermediate (HSSC): 40% of your Inter percentage
  • NET (Entry Test): 50% of your NET percentage

Formula: Aggregate = (Matric % × 0.10) + (Inter % × 0.40) + (NET % × 0.50)

Example Calculation

Suppose a student has the following scores:

  • Matric: 950 / 1100 = 86.36%
  • Intermediate: 920 / 1100 = 83.64%
  • NET: 140 / 200 = 70.00%

Aggregate = (86.36 × 0.10) + (83.64 × 0.40) + (70.00 × 0.50)

= 8.64 + 33.45 + 35.00 = 77.09%

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good NUST aggregate?

A competitive aggregate varies by program. Engineering programs typically require 70%+, while CS and business programs may have different cut-offs. Check NUST's official website for latest merit lists.

Does NUST use the same formula for all programs?

The 10-40-50 formula applies to most undergraduate engineering and science programs. Some programs like BBA or medical may have different weightages.
