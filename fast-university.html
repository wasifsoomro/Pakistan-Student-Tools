FAST-NUCES Aggregate Calculator 2026

Calculate your FAST-NUCES admission aggregate using the commonly used admission formula. FAST (Foundation for Advancement of Science and Technology) National University of Computer and Emerging Sciences uses NAT (National Aptitude Test) scores for admissions.

About FAST-NUCES Admissions

FAST-NUCES is one of Pakistan's top universities for Computer Science, Software Engineering, Electrical Engineering, and Business programs. FAST has campuses in:

  • Islamabad
  • Lahore
  • Karachi
  • Peshawar
  • Faisalabad
  • Chiniot-Faisalabad

Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum 60% marks in Matric and Inter
  • Minimum 50% in NAT-I test
  • Valid NAT score from NTS

Example Calculation

Matric: 950/1100 (86.36%) | Inter: 920/1100 (83.64%) | NAT: 78/100 (78%)

Aggregate = (86.36 × 0.10) + (83.64 × 0.40) + (78 × 0.50) = 8.64 + 33.45 + 39 = 81.09%

Frequently Asked Questions

What NAT score do I need for FAST?

You need minimum 50% in NAT-I. For competitive programs like CS at Islamabad campus, 70%+ is recommended.

Does FAST accept SAT scores?

Yes, FAST accepts SAT scores as an alternative to NAT for some programs. Check the latest admission advertisement.

Which NAT test should I take?

Take NAT-I (for pre-engineering/computer science) or NAT-IA (for business programs).

How often is the NAT test conducted?

NTS conducts the NAT test multiple times a year. Students can take the test as many times as they want and use their best score for FAST admissions.

What programs does FAST offer?

FAST offers BS in Computer Science, Software Engineering, Electrical Engineering, BBA, BS Accounting & Finance, and several other programs across its campuses.
